## 2. VISION & PHILOSOPHY

Masters' Union was established with the vision of filling the gap between conventional business education and the fast-changing demands of the global economy. The institution's core philosophy is **"Learn by Doing"** — education through real-world business practice, live projects, startup incubation, and industry immersion rather than theoretical instruction alone.

The tagline **"Learn 'Practically' by Building Real Businesses"** summarises the academic approach. Students run actual e-commerce stores, manage a real investment fund, consult for companies, and pitch startups to venture capitalists — all as part of the curriculum.

## 5. ADMISSIONS PROCESS (PGP TBM)

**Step 1:** Online application (Fee: ₹3,000–₹4,000)  
**Step 2:** Submit MU-BAAT / CAT / GMAT / GRE score + video responses  
**Step 3:** Personal Interview (30 minutes, in-person at Gurugram or online) — conducted by faculty or CXOs  
**Step 4:** Admission Decision (within 4 weeks) — Accepted / Waitlisted / Rejected  
**Step 5:** Offer Acceptance — Pay ₹90,000 (Rounds 1 & 2) or ₹1,25,000 (Round 3)  
**Step 6:** Report to campus, pay remaining fee ₹32,75,000, complete document verification  

**Admission Rounds (2025 Batch Example):**
- Round 1: Application deadline December 15 → Interviews January
- Round 2: Application deadline February 2 → Interviews February–March
- Round 3: Application deadline March 23 → Interviews April
- Batch starts: June

## 21. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ-Style Training Data)

**Q: Is CAT mandatory for Masters' Union PGP TBM admission?**  
A: No. CAT is not mandatory. Candidates can submit scores from GMAT, GRE, or take the Masters' Union Business Aptitude and Assessment Test (MU-BAAT). A CAT score of 90+ percentile strengthens but does not guarantee admission.

**Q: What is the total fee for PGP TBM?**  
A: Approximately ₹33.65 – ₹34 lakh for the full 16-month programme.

**Q: What is the total fee for the UG programmes?**  
A: ₹52,50,000 for the full 4-year course (as of 2025). Year 1 fee is approximately ₹10.80 lakh.

**Q: Does Masters' Union offer international placements?**  
A: Yes. In 2025, 30+ international offers were made. International recruiters include companies in Dubai, USA, Ireland, and other locations.

**Q: What is the acceptance rate at Masters' Union?**  
A: Approximately 10–15%, making it highly competitive.

**Q: Is the PGP TBM equivalent to an MBA?**  
A: Yes. The 16-month PGP TBM is considered equivalent to an MBA and is recognised in the industry as such.

**Q: Who are notable mentors at Masters' Union?**  
A: Edward Rogers (former Chief Knowledge Officer at NASA), Dr. Naveen Munjal (MD, Hero Electric), Rajat Mathur (MD, Morgan Stanley), and 500+ others on the mentorship platform.

**Q: What is the hostel fee?**  
A: ₹20,000 per month + GST. Amenities include pool, jacuzzi, shuttle service.

**Q: Does Masters' Union have a dual-degree programme?**  
A: Yes. The 3+1 model allows students to earn degrees from Illinois Institute of Technology (USA) or Griffith University (Australia) alongside their Indian degree.

**Q: What is the student investment fund?**  
A: Students manage a real ₹5 Crore investment fund investing across equities, startups, crypto, real estate, and commodities. The fund generated 65%+ returns in 2024–25.

**Q: Is Masters' Union AICTE approved?**  
A: Yes, it is affiliated with AICTE. It is also a member of EFMD Global and has BSIS partnerships.

**Q: Where is Masters' Union located?**  
A: DLF Cyberpark, Phase II, Udyog Vihar, Sector 20, Gurugram, Haryana, surrounded by 600+ MNCs.

**Q: Who founded Masters' Union?**  
A: Pratham Mittal, who is also the founder of Tetr College of Business.

**Q: Does Masters' Union have an online application process?**  
A: Yes. Applications can be submitted online with payment via UPI, Credit Card, Net Banking, Debit Card, and UPI. Application fee is ₹3,000–₹4,000.

**Q: What clubs and fests does Masters' Union have?**  
A: Case Union (case competition), UnMute (cultural/debate fest), Gastronomy Club, Music Club, Dance Club, SEVA (social impact), and various business clubs.
